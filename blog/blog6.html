Alzheimer: 3 Stages Of Alzheimer

Introduction

Alzheimer's disease is a neurological illness which lowers the ability of the patient to think and malfunction. This disorder has severe effects on:

  1. Memory
  2. Communication skills
  3. Character evaluation
  4. a common understanding

Alzheimer's disease primarily affects those over the age of 60. Alzheimer's disease (dementia) symptoms include a loss of capacity to speak, progressive memory loss, behavioral issues, and lack of reflection. This disorder's signs and symptoms are split into three categories:

Early Stage of Alzheimer

Difficulty learning something new, being difficult to explain to, or developing plans and activities.

2. Intermediate Stage of Alzheimer

Reduced thinking power, memory problems, mood changes, and anxiety are all common symptoms.

3. Final Stage of Alzheimer

Reduced daily tasks, such as how to dress and eat.

There are numerous ways to deal with Alzheimer's disease, including taking medication on a regular basis, but you should first check with your Neurologist.
