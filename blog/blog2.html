Migraine: Why Is It So Deadly?

Introduction

Migraine Is A Prevalent Neurological Disorder That Affects Up To 23% Of The Global Population. Patients Suffer From Headaches On A Regular Basis, Sometimes For More Than 15 Days Per Month. Migraine Headaches Can Have A Variety Of Characteristics, Such As A Full Or Half-Head Headache. It Might Be Either A Full Or Half-Head Headache. With The Combination Of Nausea And Vomiting, It Can Be Rather Severe At Times.

What are its risk factors?

Migraine is caused by a gene in the family, and its risk factors include not getting enough sleep before your period, drinking red wine and champaign, eating chocolates, sausage, strong odors like perfume, fresh paint, and a variety of other things. Aside from physical considerations, psychological elements such as joy and stress may have a role.

Migraine is most common on the weekend, when you are transitioning from a weekday of feeling constantly under pressure to a weekend of feeling more relaxed.

How do we treat Migraine?

You can try background treatment or crisis treatment if you have Migraine once a week. Its goal is to lessen the frequency and severity of the attacks, and you should seek treatment as soon as feasible. Paracetamol, aspirin, and other more specific treatments such as Triptans, the most well-known of which is Sumatriptan, are some of the recommended treatments.

If the discomfort is too severe or frequent, another option is to seek therapy from a Neurologist. It's crucial to know the difference between Migraine and a headache because the two might have quite distinct negative effects. Cluster headaches can be excruciatingly painful, but they cannot be treated in the same way that migraines are. Facial neuralgia is also one of the most excruciating types of headaches, necessitating a diagnosis and subsequent testing, such as a CT scan.
